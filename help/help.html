Become a Foster Parent

Give a pet a temporary home and help them adjust until they find a forever family.

Foster FAQ

  • Q: How long is the foster period?
    A: Usually 2–6 weeks.
  • Q: Do I need experience?
    A: No, guidance is provided.
  • Q: What does it mean to foster a pet?
    A: You provide a temporary, safe home for an animal, helping them prepare for their forever family.
  • Q: How long is a typical foster period?
    A: The duration varies, but it is usually 2–6 weeks.
  • Q: Do I need to have prior experience with animals?
    A: No, previous experience is not required. We provide all the necessary guidance and support.
  • Q: What supplies will I need to provide?
    A: You're typically responsible for food and toys. The rescue will often provide a crate, bedding, and medical supplies.
  • Q: What are my responsibilities as a foster parent?
    A: Your main roles are providing food, water, shelter, and socialization. The rescue organization covers all veterinary care.
  • Q: What if the pet gets sick or injured?
    A: The rescue organization is responsible for all medical care. You should contact us immediately for instructions.
  • Q: Can I foster if I already have my own pets?
    A: Yes, but you must ensure your pets are up-to-date on vaccinations. We will help you with a safe introduction plan.
  • Q: Can I adopt the pet I'm fostering?
    A: Yes! Many foster parents decide to adopt their foster pets, and we call this a "foster fail"—a happy ending for everyone.

"Fostering has been so rewarding! You really do save lives." - Asha, Foster Parent
